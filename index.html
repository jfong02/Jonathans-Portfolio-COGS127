About Me

I'm a third year Cognitive Science student at UC San Diego pursuing a B.S. in Design and Human-Computer Interactions. Some courses I've taken involve the study of neuroscience, human cognition and computer programming as well as applications in team-based design centered courses. When I'm not coding or creating wireframes and storyboards, you'll find me playing violin and guitar and trying out new places to eat around town.

Why Design?

To me, design is not just how to make the best looking interactive app, or the product that has the most impressive features. It’s really about finding out specific problems and needs that users and clients have, and addressing those problems in a variety of different ways. I’ve learned that having a need-based mindset to find solutions in various aspects of the workplace and at home is essential for gaining insight into ways to increase efficiency and understanding in how data and information is better processed, understood, and utilized.

My Story

During my undergraduate education studying the wide, interdisciplinary scope of cognitive science coupled with my specialization of design and interaction, I experienced how the design process is centered on a need-based problem-finding mindset in various aspects of our daily lives. I have been exposed to many areas of the design process, from needfinding and ideating, to creating personas and storyboards, to different degrees of fidelity in prototyping, to implementation and coding.

These skills have been applied in several projects, including a mobile app to connect volunteers and the elderly to assist the elderly in their grocery needs, which you can check out my design process details here or click the top logo, as well as a mobile site to improve college students’ cooking experience by utilizing leftover groceries in the kitchen and finding matching recipes in an intuitive and easy to use site. You can find out more about the project prototype site here or the bottom logo.
